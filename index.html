About Me

I am Aybüke Küçük, a Geomatics Engineering student at Hacettepe University with a strong interest in mapping technologies, spatial data analysis, and modern geospatial tools. During my studies, I have built a solid background in cartography, GNSS positioning, and remote sensing. I am particularly passionate about photogrammetry and data science, as I enjoy exploring how spatial data can be processed, analyzed, and visualized to solve real-world problems.

I have completed internships at Mipmap Technologies and Adana Water and Sewerage Administration (ASKİ). At Mipmap, I was involved in photogrammetry and remote sensing projects, gaining hands-on experience with image-based mapping and geospatial data workflows. At ASKİ, I had the opportunity to gain field experience in geodesy and surveying, which helped me better understand the practical aspects of geomatics engineering.

My goal is to combine my technical background and analytical mindset to develop innovative geospatial solutions that bridge the gap between fieldwork and data-driven decision-making.
